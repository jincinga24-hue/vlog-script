Analysis — Retrospective & Key Concepts
~40 sec
Through our team retrospective, reflecting on our Miro board — our SWOT analyses, archetype roles, and team charter — I was able to critically assess what happened. PAUSE

In the High-Performing Teams Masterclass, we learned about Radical Candor — Kim Scott's framework where high-performing teams need to both care personally and challenge directly. When you care but don't challenge, that's called Ruinous Empathy. PAUSE And that's exactly where I was — so focused on keeping things positive that I avoided raising a real concern. CAMERA Recognising that shift — from Ruinous Empathy to Radical Candor — was a turning point. As a Giver, my value isn't just in being supportive. It's in being supportive and honest.

TONE The CRM challenge reinforced this. Proskill has 4,000 contacts in their CRM with 2,000 active customers — and our goal was to use AI to segment them, predict demand, and automate outreach. But none of us had worked with CRM platforms before. SLOW The gap between what we scoped and what we could deliver was a real challenge I hadn't anticipated in consulting.

Response, Growth & Action Plan
~50 sec
If I could do it again, I would have spoken up immediately in that meeting with Chris, rather than processing it quietly. PAUSE But what I did do was bring it to the team — and we had a productive discussion about how to navigate the ACL and greenwashing risks while still communicating Proskill's genuine environmental impact. That developed a new capability in me — balancing support with honest challenge.

SLOW So I want to START being more upfront about challenges in the moment. In consulting, early honesty saves time.

PAUSE

On the CRM gap — rather than waiting, I signed up for Salesforce Trailhead and completed modules on CRM analytics, customer segmentation, and lead scoring — which directly relates to how we'll work with Proskill's 4,000-contact database. CAMERA That shift — from supporting the team emotionally to also technically — is a capability I want to carry into my professional career.

PAUSE

I want to STOP letting my effectiveness fade over time. My SWOT identified this — I start strong but that energy can drop off. I need to stay intentional about consistency.

TONE And I'll CONTINUE investing in team trust. Our retrospective showed that the reason we navigated these challenges was because we had psychological safety — and as the Giver, that's my contribution.
