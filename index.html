Evaluation & Analysis
~60 sec
EVALUATION Through our team retrospective, we agreed on two actions: setting clear milestones with deadlines, and clearly defining task ownership. Kim and I learned this the hard way — we both independently researched the same prospects, ending up with duplicate work. The issue wasn't effort — it was communication. PAUSE

ANALYSIS This connects to Radical Candor from the High-Performing Teams Masterclass — caring personally while challenging directly. CAMERA An wanted to add Masterclass 2 content into our retrospective, and part of me wanted to say yes. But the timeline was tight, and my SWOT weakness is overcommitting — saying yes until quality drops. PAUSE

SLOW So I told An — I appreciate your work, but adding more might stretch us too thin. That was uncomfortable — my sensitivity means I worry about reactions. But An thanked me for being honest — she'd been thinking the same thing. PAUSE That was my turning point — from Ruinous Empathy to Radical Candor. As a Giver, my value isn't just being supportive — it's being supportive and honest. In industry, this separates someone who avoids hard conversations from a trusted advisor.

Conclusion & Action Plan
~50 sec
CONCLUSION If I could redo it, I'd speak up immediately with Chris instead of going quiet — I didn't trust my judgment in front of the client yet. But I did bring it to the team, and we reached a better approach.

The prospect research reinforced this. Chris asked us to find 100 prospect contacts — names, emails, LinkedIn profiles. We started manually; after half an hour, we had five. PAUSE So I learned to use Claude to pull contact data. By next morning, we had all 100. CAMERA That shift — from supporting the team emotionally to technically — is a capability I'll carry into my career. In consulting, clients don't wait for you to be ready.

ACTION START — preparing one critical question before every client meeting, so I speak up in the moment.

STOP — letting my effectiveness fade. My SWOT flagged this, so I'm setting a weekly self-review to hold myself to the standard I set in week one.

TONE CONTINUE — investing in team trust. The reason we navigated these challenges was psychological safety — and as the Giver, that's my contribution.

SLOW CAMERA The biggest lesson — consulting isn't about having all the answers. It's about being honest when you don't, and having the initiative to find them.

Thanks for watching.
